Terms & Conditions


Level One Advertising
Level One offers a moderate level of advertising implementation into the car's system. This includes the infotainment screen and the car's audio system. The displayed advertisements will never be in the driver's way of operating the vehicle safely. Additionally, as the level One offers no personalisation, no data will be shared with TOL for the purpose of advertising. This will result in less relevant advertising being shown.

Level Two Advertising
Level Two offers more advertising within the system compared to Level One. It again uses both the infotainment system screen and the vehicle's audio system. As Level Two operates with higher personalisation, the vehicle will collect & send relevant data, including snippets from the vehicle's internal microphones. This function can be temporarily disabled in the car's settings, however it will turn on automatically after five minutes and on start. None of the advertising features will prevent the driver from safe operation of the vehicle.

General
By selecting one of the options above the customer agrees to keeping and using the vehicle for at least four (4) years with minimum 5000 miles driven annually. The customer has the option to op-out of either selected advertising levels within the first two months of purchase by paying the difference to RRP.
The infotainment system runs on Google's Android Automotive platform and can therefore be connected to apps such as Spotify and YouTube. Android Auto and Apple Car Play is not available for vehicles with advertising integration enabled.
Audio system volume will automatically rise to 10 (out of 50) when an advertisement starts and the 'Mute' option is disabled for the first 5 seconds, until skipping the promotion becomes available.

Advertising will trigger even if there is no media playing or the car's cabin is quiet. It will, however postpone and queue advertisements during phone calls. Level Two has the ability to analyse conversations and will not disturb an important/serious conversation. Level One does not have this feature.

The long print


The trickiest mode, but we are keeping you safe! We know car infotainments are often criticised for moving controls to touch screens. Understandably, introducing distracting advertisements into the mix would be irresponsible. To mitigate this, the advertising in our systems never seeks more attention that other tasks on the screen. Visual advertisements have a set place within the home screen and never overlay other apps during driving. You can interact with them only to the degree any other control would require them, never requiring more than one or two taps. There aren't paragraphs of text to read, because alongside visual advertising – and as a part of it – is the audio advertising. Since the media is playing through the car’s system, the advertisement will not start mid-song or a phone call and will scan for a suitable gap in a podcast before starting, making sure you are not left on a clift hanger. If the advertisement is geographically sensitive it will give the option to reroute to the address of the business, while all offers can be saved, using buttons on the steering wheel.

All advertising is ‘skippable’ after a few seconds.
No advertising will start during a parking sequence.
